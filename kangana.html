Kangana Ranaut

Kangna Amardeep Ranaut, a prominent figure in Hindi cinema, is celebrated for her compelling portrayals of strong women in female-centric films. Her performances have earned her four National Film Awards, five Filmfare Awards, and spots in Forbes India's Celebrity 100 list six times. In 2020, the Government of India honored her with the Padma Shri for her contributions to Indian cinema. Ranaut's journey began at sixteen with modeling before training under theatre director Arvind Gaur. Her film debut in the 2006 thriller "Gangster" earned her the Filmfare Award for Best Female Debut. Subsequent emotionally intense roles in films like "Woh Lamhe..." (2006), "Life in a... Metro" (2007), and "Fashion" (2008) brought critical acclaim, with the latter earning her the National Film Award for Best Supporting Actress. Despite facing criticism for being typecast, Ranaut defied expectations with a well-received comic role in "Tanu Weds Manu" (2011). She continued her success with "Krrish 3" (2013), one of the highest-grossing Indian films. Consecutive National Film Awards for Best Actress for "Queen" (2014) and "Tanu Weds Manu Returns" (2015) followed. In 2019, her co-directed venture, "Manikarnika: The Queen of Jhansi," was a success. In 2020, she entered production with her company, Manikarnika Films, showcasing her talents as a director and producer. Known for her outspoken nature and alignment with right-wing ideologies, Ranaut's bold opinions and frequent clashes contribute to her reputation as a multifaceted and assertive personality in the industry.
