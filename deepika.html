Deepika Padukone

Deepika Padukone, a leading Indian actress dominating the Hindi film industry, stands tall as one of the highest-paid stars, boasting three Filmfare Awards. Recognized as one of India's most popular personalities, she earned global acclaim, securing a spot on Time's list of the 100 most influential people in the world in 2018 and receiving the Time100 Impact Award in 2022. Born in Copenhagen and raised in Bangalore as the daughter of badminton player Prakash Padukone, Deepika initially pursued badminton at the national level. However, her trajectory shifted from sports to become a fashion model. The Bollywood romance "Om Shanti Om" (2007), opposite Shah Rukh Khan, marked her debut in Hindi cinema and won her the Filmfare Award for Best Female Debut. While garnering acclaim for her role in "Love Aaj Kal" (2009), Deepika faced a brief setback before her career took a significant turn with the romantic comedy "Cocktail" (2012). This marked a pivotal moment, leading to further success in films like "Yeh Jawaani Hai Deewani," "Chennai Express" (both 2013), "Happy New Year" (2014), and Sanjay Leela Bhansali's period dramas "Bajirao Mastani" (2015) and "Padmaavat" (2018). Her global presence expanded with the Hollywood action film "XXX: Return of Xander Cage" (2017). Deepika earned critical acclaim for her diverse roles, including a character inspired by Juliet in "Goliyon Ki Raasleela Ram-Leela" (2013) and a headstrong daughter in "Piku" (2015), securing two Filmfare Awards for Best Actress.
